Understanding the Data

Bristol Predict scrapes daily harvest reports directly from the Alaska Department of Fish and Game, then visualizes that data with interactive charts, maps, and analysis tools. You're not seeing forecasts or predictions‚Äîyou're seeing what actually happened in Bristol Bay.

Use these insights to understand seasonal patterns, compare district performance, identify peak fishing windows, and benchmark your catch against the broader fishery. The data itself is compelling. And it's there waiting to teach you something about Bristol Bay.

Why People Use Bristol Predict

Whether you're a commercial fisher, a data enthusiast, or simply curious about Bristol Bay, there's something here worth seeing.

Real-Time Data

Daily harvest updates from Alaska Department of Fish and Game. When the 2026 fishing season begins, Bristol Predict automatically scrapes and integrates the new data. Year after year, the historical record grows. (Note: ADF&G data publicly available starts at 2023‚Äîthough 2022 was a record year for sockeye, earlier seasons remain inaccessible. Why I Built Bristol Predict

The story behind the project and the vision for what it could become.

Julian's Vision

Bristol Predict started as a personal challenge: could I build something that brings clarity to the noise? Bristol Bay's salmon data exists, but it's scattered across PDFs and government sites. The patterns are hidden.

I wanted to create a tool that didn't make promises it couldn't keep‚Äîno black-box predictions, no false confidence. Just clean data visualization, honest analysis, and the tools to discover what the numbers actually show. Something useful for commercial fishermen, interesting for data enthusiasts, and genuinely beautiful to use.

This project combines everything I love: web scraping, geospatial data, real-time visualization, and the complexity of a real-world problem. Every fishing season, Bristol Predict will automatically pull the latest data from Alaska's Department of Fish and Game, adding 2026, 2027, and beyond to the historical record. It grows with the seasons.

What started as a portfolio piece became something I genuinely believe in. The data deserves to be seen. Bristol Bay deserves tools built with care.
