Understanding the Data:

Bristol Predict scrapes daily harvest reports directly from the Alaska Department of Fish and Game, then visualizes that data with interactive charts, maps, and analysis tools.

Use these insights to understand seasonal patterns, compare district performance, identify peak fishing windows, and benchmark your catch against the broader fishery. The data itself is compelling, and it's there waiting to teach you something about Bristol Bay.

Why Use Bristol Predict

Whether you're a commercial fisherman, a data enthusiast, or simply curious about Bristol Bay, there's something here worth seeing.

Real-Time Data

Daily harvest updates from Alaska Department of Fish and Game. When the 2026 fishing season begins, Bristol Predict automatically scrapes and integrates the new data. Year after year, the historical record grows.

(Note: ADF&G only has publicly available data starting from the 2023 season. Earlier seasons' data remains inaccessible for web-scraping at this time. More history would reveal so much more...)

Why I Built Bristol Predict

Julian's Vision

I began commercial fishing in Bristol Bay at 19, and immediately understood what I was looking at: a fishery of staggering complexity. Hundreds of boats. Five districts. Different strategies. Different fish. Different openings. Every day, the data floods in. From catch numbers, to escapement reports, fishing announcements, tidal predictions, delivery numbers and so much more. There is data everywhere. Now, Bristol Bay's fishery data exists, but it's scattered across PDFs, government sites and radio reports.

I wanted to build something that could bring clarity to that noise. A single place where all of this data lived. Where patterns emerged. Where you could actually see what was happening in the fishery instead of chasing it across a dozen different sources.

I started with ambitious goals; collect the data, build a dashboard, use machine learning to find deeper patterns. Maybe even predict what would happen next. But the reality was humbling: ADF&G only began publishing daily fishery data in 2023. It was not enough. So I pivoted to something more direct. Clean visualization. Real analysis. Tools that reveal what the numbers actually show, not what I wanted them to show. This being said, Bristol Predict automatically pulls the latest data from ADF&G. 2026, 2027, seasons beyond, allowing the app grow and scale. As time passes, the historical record builds, and I believe that some day in the future, machine learning can be given this wealth of data to uncover even more.

I wanted to build something useful for commercial fishermen. Something interesting for data enthusiasts. And something genuinely beautiful to look at. This project combines everything I love: web-development, cartography, data visualization, complexities of a real-world problem, and most importantly, a fishery that has become a huge part of my life.

This data deserves to be seen. The Bristol Bay fishery deserves modern tools built with care.
